Back to main page There are two main network types, Wide Area Network (WAN) and Local Area Network (LAN)

1. Local Area Newtworks

Local area networks are networks that occupy a small geographic area, examples include your house, school or workplace, you own all equipment within the LAN such as your router, any switches and wireless access points that you have. Your internet connection will typically be provided by an Internet Service Provider (ISP) and you will have a subnet mask applied to your ip address which dictates how much of your address you own, and can assign to the various devices in your network. For example a subnet mask of 255.255.255.0 means that you own the final section of the ip and you can change that section.

2. Wide Area Networks

Wide area networks are the networks that connect all of the Local Area Networks in a given area, Wide Area Networks Cover a large Geographical area typically a town or county. These are typically managed by Internet Service Providers.
